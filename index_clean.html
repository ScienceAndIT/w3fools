W3Fools

A Takedown of W3Schools

A few of us JavaScript developers have been collecting cases where w3schools.com contains inaccurate information or just really bad advice. We certainly do not advocate that anyone use w3schools as a reference or place to learn techniques.The Mozilla Docs Center (MDC) is much much better[a]. It is also a wiki (little known fact), which means we, as knowledgeable web developers, can add or change information so the pages are as effective as possible.

This website is available on github, if you'd like to contribute, just fork this repository and send pull requests. ~ paul irish, January 7th, 2011

Did you know?

  1. W3Schools.com is absolutely not affiliated with the W3C. In fact the W3C has asked them to disavow any connection by explicitly stating they are unrelated. W3Schools refused. (src?)
  2. W3Schools publishes inaccurate, misleading, and confusing content. Just scroll down this page. We dare you. [c]
  3. W3Schools sell their certificates (for $95), which actually have no value, because they’re not affiliated with any educational organizations/institutions. (src?)

We believe w3schools is harmful to the web. Web developers deserve better.

Goals

  1. Shame.
  2. More people to know they have NO RELATION to W3C. New users know w3schools is not official and their certificates are not valid (a lot of them with not enough grasp of English purchase these certificates which are not that cheap). Make sure it does not land in any top results for searches on writing good markup, style and script.
  3. Better cognizance that w3schools is riddled with bad information. We urge everyone, not just web developers, to stop linking/reference w3schools. [d]
  4. Along with promoteJS, sharing the fact that MDC is the best resource for JavaScript and CSS. For HTML, we should from now on use www.w3.org/html/wiki/Learn, see also www.w3.org/html/wiki/Elements. [e]
  5. W3schools to consider wikifying/github-ing their content [f] so community will self correct and keep the information up-to-date. Today, they do not even allow you to submit corrections on a page.
