W3Schools is trouble

  1. W3Schools.com is not affiliated with the W3C. In fact the W3C has asked them to disavow any connection by explicitly stating they are unrelated. W3Schools refused.
  2. W3Schools sell their certificates (for $95), which actually have no value, because they're not affiliated with any educational organizations/institutions nor do they develop the languages, standards they sell the certifications for.
  3. W3Schools publishes inaccurate, misleading, and confusing content. Just scroll down this page. We dare you. [c]

We believe w3schools is harmful to the web. Web developers deserve better.

Why does it matter?

Bad education hurts.

  1. Being educated poorly hampers your ability to score a good job.
  2. Inaccurate references slow development and cause costly QA loops.
  3. Learning key web development idioms slowly or wrongly puts you years behind your own colleagues.

What should be done

  1. Learn from reputable sources.
    • HTML Dog is a fantastic and comprehensive intro for HTML and CSS.
    • Opera Web Standards Curriculum covers the basics of web standards-based design in HTML and CSS.
    • The MDC (Mozilla's Doc Center) takes over at intermediate CSS and covers JavaScript better than anyone.
      • The MDC is also a wiki (little known fact), which means we, as knowledgeable web developers, can add or change information so the pages are as effective
    • The W3C has a wiki-based general Learn page as well as an HTML element reference.
  2. W3schools should consider wikifying their content so the community would self correct and keep the information up-to-date. Today, they do not even allow you to submit corrections on a page. They should.
